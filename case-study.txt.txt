Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is one of the most common cyber crimes, where hackers try to obtain sensitive information from a victim by pretending to be a trusted source. This usually occurs when a person receives an email or message from a trusted source, asking them to click on a link and verify their account information.

In one instance, a student received a message saying that his or her account was going to be blocked if he or she did not update his or her information immediately. The message had a link that seemed to be from the bank’s official website. Without verifying, he or she entered his or her login credentials and OTP. Within a matter of minutes, money was withdrawn from his or her account without his or her knowledge.

This happened because the attacker had created a fake website that looked exactly the same as the original website. The victim had been tricked into believing the message because of the sense of urgency it carried.

This case demonstrates the need to be careful while surfing the internet. A person should be aware of the source of the message and should not click on the links. This would have helped to prevent the crime.
